## Appendix C: Sideways — A Brief Note

This appendix was to contain a 4,000-word analysis of lateral gravitational theory, but due to a printing error, it was bound into a completely different book (*Passion on Procyon IV*, Conditions Press, Maximegalon). Readers seeking the sideways analysis should purchase that book and ignore everything between pages 47 and 93 that involves Commander Thrang and the Ambassador's daughter. The gravitational material is on pages 94-127, between a love scene and a recipe for soup.

We apologise for the inconvenience.[^1]

[^1]: We do not, in fact, apologise. We simply say we do. This is standard publishing practice and, we note, also the standard divine response to most complaints about Creation.

## Appendix D: A Complete List of Things That Work Properly

1. Quarks
2. Dogs
3. The mathematics of music
4. Friday evenings
5. The colour blue[^2]
6. Toast (when it doesn't fall butter-side down, which is most of the time, despite what people claim)[^3]
7. Sleep (the concept, not the execution, which is riddled with problems)
8. The smell of rain
9. Otters holding hands while sleeping so they don't drift apart

That is the complete list.

[^2]: Blue is genuinely well-designed. It works at every scale — the sky, the ocean, a nice mug. Most colours have contexts where they fail. Blue does not. If the entire universe had been designed with the same care as blue, this book would not exist.

[^3]: The "toast always falls butter-side down" claim has been extensively studied and found to be only partially true. Toast falls butter-side down approximately 62% of the time, which is more than chance but less than conspiracy. It is, like most things in the universe, slightly worse than it should be but not so much worse that you can prove deliberate malice. This is the zone in which the universe operates: the narrow band between "acceptable" and "actionable."
