# Appendix A: Selected Correspondence

*Being a representative sample of letters received following the publication of "Where God Went Wrong," together with the author's replies.*

---

## Letter 1

**From:** The Most Reverend Halfrunt of Betelgeuse
**To:** Professor Oolon Colluphil, University of Maximegalon
**Date:** 3rd Day of the Bewildered Donkey, Galactic Year 2023

Dear Professor Colluphil,

I write to express my profound dismay at the publication of your so-called "academic" work, *Where God Went Wrong*. In forty years of theological practice, I have never encountered a more irresponsible, more reckless, or more comprehensively wrong-headed piece of writing. Your arguments are shallow, your evidence is selective, and your tone is that of a man who has mistaken rudeness for rigour.

God does not make mistakes. God's creation is perfect. The fact that you cannot perceive this perfection is a failure of your perception, not of God's work. I urge you, in the strongest possible terms, to retract this book, apologise to the faithful, and pursue a line of inquiry more suited to your limited capabilities.

Yours in faith and considerable irritation,
The Most Rev. Halfrunt

---

**Reply:**

Dear Reverend Halfrunt,

Thank you for your letter, which I have read with the same attention you gave my book — which is to say, selectively and with predetermined conclusions.

You write that "God does not make mistakes." You then identify a flaw in my perception. My perception was created by God. If my perception is flawed, God made a mistake. If my perception is not flawed, my book is correct.

I look forward to your resolution of this dilemma, though I suspect I will be looking forward for some time.

Warm regards,
Oolon Colluphil

P.S. — Your letter contained three spelling errors. I assume these, too, are evidence of God's perfection.

## Letter 2

**From:** The Lamuella Fundamentalist Church (Committee for Doctrinal Purity)
**To:** The Heretic Colluphil
**Date:** Undated (arrived with scorch marks)

HERETIC,

You will BURN for your BLASPHEMY. The Almighty will SMITE you and your WICKED BOOK will be CONSIGNED to the FLAMES of ETERNAL—

[The remainder of this letter is illegible due to the author's apparent difficulty with both theology and penmanship. Several words appear to have been crossed out and rewritten multiple times. The word "damnation" is spelled four different ways, none of them correctly.]

---

**Reply:**

Dear Committee,

I appreciate the energy, if not the spelling.

You threaten me with burning. I note that fire is an exothermic chemical reaction involving the rapid oxidation of a fuel source — a process governed by the laws of thermodynamics, which are themselves among the poorly calibrated systems documented in my book. If God smites me with fire, He will be using one of His own mistakes as a weapon, which rather undermines the message.

Additionally, I note that your letter arrived with scorch marks, suggesting that you attempted to set it on fire before posting it. This is not how the postal system works. This is, however, an excellent metaphor for theology: setting your own arguments on fire and then blaming the recipient.

Cordially,
Oolon Colluphil

## Letter 3

**From:** Dr. Qual Hesterton, Betelgeuse (formerly Colluphil's therapist)
**To:** Professor Colluphil
**Date:** 17th Day of the Uncertain Rabbit

Dear Oolon,

I am writing to inform you that I can no longer serve as your therapist. Our sessions, which were intended to address your anxiety and existential dread, have instead deepened my own. I now lie awake at night thinking about the giraffe's laryngeal nerve. I have developed a persistent awareness of my own digestive system that I find profoundly distressing. Last Tuesday, I stubbed my toe and, instead of simply saying "ow," I delivered a fifteen-minute lecture to my empty kitchen about nociceptor distribution in the human foot.

You have not been cured. I have been infected.

I wish you well, at a considerable distance.

Dr. Qual Hesterton

P.S. — My back went out while writing this letter. I thought of you immediately.

---

**Reply:**

Dear Qual,

I am sorry to lose you as a therapist, though I note with some satisfaction that you now understand me completely.

You describe your awareness of the giraffe's laryngeal nerve as a source of distress. I describe it as enlightenment. The difference between distress and enlightenment is, in my experience, largely a matter of tenure.

You are welcome to cite me in any future publications. I will cite you in mine. Your observation about the stomach and the bridge has made it into Chapter 2, where it will outlive us both — though given the state of our respective backs, this is not as impressive as it sounds.

Get well soon, and if you cannot get well, at least get footnoted.

Fondly,
Oolon

## Letter 4

**From:** Anonymous (later identified as the Dean of Maximegalon Seminary)
**To:** Professor Colluphil
**Date:** Confidential

Dear Professor Colluphil,

I am writing anonymously because my position does not permit me to agree with you publicly, and yet I find myself unable to disagree with you privately.

Your book is, damn it, annoyingly well-argued. I have spent thirty years teaching the perfection of divine creation, and your documentation of the human knee alone has forced me to restructure my entire Introductory Theology syllabus. My students are asking questions I cannot answer. My colleagues are avoiding eye contact. Last Sunday, during a sermon on the wonder of the human body, I accidentally said "the wonder of the human body, such as it is," and the congregation noticed.

I prayed for a rebuttal. I received only silence. This, as you would no doubt observe, rather proves your point.

I enclose a donation to your research fund. Please do not acknowledge it publicly.

Anonymous

---

**Reply:**

Dear Dean (and yes, I know it's you — your stationery has the Seminary watermark, and you're the only person on Maximegalon who uses the phrase "damn it" as a comma),

Your donation is received with gratitude and your anonymity is respected, except insofar as I have just identified you in a letter that will be published in my next book. I apologise for this. I also predict that you will not mind as much as you think you will, because the relief of finally admitting the truth — even accidentally, even in print — is considerably greater than the discomfort of being caught.

Welcome to the right side of history. The view is terrible, but at least it's honest.

Your colleague in reluctant truth,
Oolon Colluphil

## Letter 5

**From:** Prostetnic Vogon Jeltz
**To:** O. Colluphil, Author
**Date:** Stamped with seventeen official seals

RE: YOUR BOOK (Reference Number: VBR-4412-C/7/SubParagraph 9.2.1)

I have read your book as part of my duties reviewing all published materials in Sector ZZ9 for compliance with Vogon Literary Standards (Form 27B/6, as amended).

Your book fails to comply with Section 4, Paragraph 12 of the Standards, which requires all published works to include a completed Environmental Impact Assessment, a Structural Integrity Report, and a Notarised Declaration of Pointlessness.

However, I note that your chapter on the Vogons contains several inaccuracies. You describe Vogon poetry as "terrible." This is incorrect. Vogon poetry is *thorough*. You describe Vogon bureaucracy as "excessive." This is also incorrect. Vogon bureaucracy is *complete*. There is a difference.

I have enclosed the appropriate correction forms (27B/Stroke 6, in triplicate) and a copy of my latest poem, "Ode to a Requisition Form (Part 47 of 312)."

Failure to file corrections within forty-seven working days will result in the demolition of your office.

Prostetnic Vogon Jeltz
Third Administrative Division

---

**Reply:**

Dear Prostetnic Vogon Jeltz,

I have received your correction forms and your poem.

The correction forms have been filed. The poem has been used to file them — specifically, as kindling, which I feel represents the poem's highest and most useful purpose.

You describe your poetry as "thorough." I have forwarded this claim to the Galactic Arms Limitation Committee, who will no doubt wish to assess whether "thoroughness" constitutes a mitigating or aggravating factor in the classification of your verses as weapons.

Regarding the threatened demolition of my office: you will find it on the fourth floor of the Maximegalon Humanities Building, room 412. I should warn you that the building is already in a state of advanced structural decline, having been designed — much like everything else in this universe — without adequate planning, materials, or quality assurance. You may find that demolition is unnecessary. The building is doing it to itself.

Much like the universe.

Architecturally yours,
Oolon Colluphil

P.S. — I have not completed the Environmental Impact Assessment, on the grounds that the environment was already impacted before I got here, and my book is merely documenting the damage.

## Letter 6

**From:** A delegation of dolphins (translated)
**To:** The land person who wrote the book about the obvious things
**Date:** [Series of clicks interpreted as "time is a flat circle and calendars are a land invention we find quaint"]

We have read your book.

We found it adequate.

You have identified several problems that we identified approximately forty million years ago. We considered writing a similar book but decided it was beneath us, as the problems are self-evident to anyone paying attention, which land-dwellers generally are not.

Your chapter on pain was, we concede, reasonably observed. Your chapter on weather demonstrated awareness, if not sophistication. Your treatment of the digestive system was competent.

We do not write to praise you. We write to inform you that you have missed the biggest mistake of all, which we will not identify, because it is more amusing to watch you continue searching.

So long, and thanks for all the footnotes.

The dolphins

---

**Reply:**

Dear Dolphins,

Thank you for your feedback, which manages to be simultaneously encouraging and infuriating — a quality I have come to associate with beings who know more than they're willing to share.

You say I have "missed the biggest mistake of all." This is either a profound observation or an elaborate tease. I suspect it is both.

I will continue searching. You will continue watching. The arrangement is, like everything else in this universe, not ideal but apparently permanent.

With respect and mild exasperation,
Oolon Colluphil

P.S. — What is it? Seriously. What is the biggest mistake?

P.P.S. — I know you won't tell me. This, too, is consistent with the general management style of the universe.
