# Chapter 2: The Curriculum Vitae

*In which the author examines God's qualifications for the position of Supreme Being and finds them, to put it charitably, thin.*

---

If God were applying for a job — and I maintain that He is, perpetually, whether He knows it or not, because the position of "Creator and Sustainer of All Existence" is not a tenure-track appointment; it must be earned daily — then at some point, someone would have to look at His CV.

No one ever has.

This is remarkable. We require curriculum vitae from plumbers. We require them from accountants, teachers, sous-chefs, and the people who operate the small rides at traveling fairs. We require documented proof of competence from virtually everyone who proposes to do anything of any significance to anyone, up to and including the man who trims the hedges outside the Sidereal Institute.

But the being who claims to have *created hedges* — and also the Institute, and also the planet on which they both sit, and also the concept of sitting — has never once been asked to demonstrate that He is qualified for the role.[^1]

Until now.

I have compiled, from available sources — religious texts, creation myths, observed physical laws, and one very confused burning bush that I interviewed on Sector ZZ9 Plural Z Alpha — a comprehensive curriculum vitae for the Supreme Being. I present it here, exactly as the evidence supports:

---

**CURRICULUM VITAE**

**Name:** God (also Yahweh, Allah, Brahma, the Almighty, the Creator, "Him Upstairs," and, in certain circles, "That Bastard")

**Address:** Everywhere, allegedly. No fixed abode confirmed. Multiple attempts to deliver post have failed. The postal service reports that letters addressed to "God, Heaven" are returned marked "INSUFFICIENT ADDRESS," which is ironic for someone who supposedly encompasses all of spacetime.

**Date of Birth:** Unknown. Claims to predate time itself, which is either profoundly impressive or profoundly suspicious, depending on your tolerance for unverifiable claims on job applications.[^2]

**Education:** Self-taught. No formal qualifications. No degrees, diplomas, or certificates of any kind. Claims to be the source of all knowledge, which is the sort of thing people say when they don't have a degree.

**Work Experience:**

- **Creator of the Universe** (Dates: Beginning of Time — Present)
  - Responsibilities: Everything.
  - Achievements: Created matter, energy, spacetime, and life. Also created death, suffering, the common cold, and that thing where you walk into a room and forget why you came in.
  - References: None provided.

- **Previous Employment:** None listed. Claims to have existed eternally prior to creating the universe, which raises the question of what He was doing before that. Eternally. The answer appears to be: nothing. He was doing nothing, forever, and then suddenly decided to create everything. This is the résumé of someone who spent an infinite amount of time on the sofa and then had a sudden burst of manic productivity.[^3]

**Skills:**

- **Omniscience** — Self-assessed. Unverified by any independent body. If God is omniscient, He knew this book would be written, He knew what it would say, and He applied for the job anyway, which suggests either supreme confidence or the kind of self-delusion you usually only see in candidates who list "perfectionist" as a weakness.

- **Omnipotence** — Self-assessed. Supposedly capable of anything, including creating a rock so heavy He cannot lift it, which is not so much a paradox as a failed interview question. Any competent hiring manager would have flagged this.

- **Omnipresence** — Self-assessed. Claims to be everywhere at once. Has nevertheless been conspicuously absent from: wars, famines, plagues, the collapse of the Hrung, and every single philosophy department in every single university on every single planet that has ever asked Him to pop in for a chat.

- **Communication** — Poor. Primary communication methods include: burning shrubbery, dreams (indistinguishable from normal dreams), stone tablets (delivered once, never followed up), and "a still, small voice" (indistinguishable from one's own thoughts). Has not issued a press release in approximately two thousand years. In any other field, this would be considered a resignation.

- **Management** — See "the entire history of everything." Performance review: unsatisfactory.

- **Delegation** — Extensive. Has delegated virtually all responsibilities to: prophets (unreliable), angels (middle management, prone to rebellion), and human beings (catastrophically unqualified). This is not delegation. This is abandonment.

**Interests:** Unknown. If the creation is any indication: beetles (created approximately 400,000 species), darkness (created five times more dark matter than regular matter), and irony (created beings capable of questioning His existence, then made His existence questionable).

**Referees:** None. Has worked alone for the entirety of existence. No colleagues, no supervisors, no performance reviews. The only being in history to hold a position of absolute authority with zero accountability. This is not a god. This is a dictator with good PR.

---

Now. Let us consider this CV as a hiring manager would.

The candidate has one item of work experience: the universe. It is, admittedly, a large project. But it is also the *only* project, which means we have no way of knowing whether it's any good, because we have nothing to compare it to. It's rather like an artist who has painted exactly one painting and insists it's a masterpiece. Perhaps it is. But you'd feel more confident if they'd done a few sketches first.

The references are a problem. Every job application requires references. Every single one. The teenager applying for a weekend position at the chip shop must produce someone who will vouch for their character. God has produced no one. In billions of years, across an entire universe teeming with life, He has not cultivated a single professional relationship that He is willing to put on paper.

This tells us one of two things. Either everyone who has worked with God has such a low opinion of Him that He dare not ask them for a reference — which is damning. Or there is no one to ask — which is more damning still, because it means He has been working alone, in the dark, with no feedback, no quality control, and no one to say, "Are you sure about the wasp?"

The skills are self-assessed. Every single one. Omniscience, omnipotence, omnipresence — all claimed, none verified. In any other context, a candidate who listed three "omni-" level skills with no supporting evidence would be gently escorted from the building. In this context, we built the building *for* Him.[^4]

The work history reveals a pattern that any experienced recruiter would recognize: a single, enormous project delivered with no planning, no testing, no iteration, and no documentation. The universe has no user manual. It has no specifications. It has no version history, no changelog, no README. It was deployed directly to production with no staging environment, and it has been running in production ever since, accumulating bugs that no one is fixing because the developer has gone silent.

This is not the CV of a Supreme Being.

This is the CV of a freelancer who took on a project that was too big, delivered it late, delivered it broken, and then ghosted the client.

And the client — that's us, by the way; we're the client — has been sitting here ever since, refreshing our inbox, waiting for a reply that is never going to come.

---

[^1]: The hedge trimmer at the Sidereal Institute, for the record, has seventeen years of experience, a Level 4 Certificate in Ornamental Topiary, and references from three previous botanical institutions. He is, by any objective measure, more qualified for his job than God is for His.

[^2]: I once interviewed a candidate who claimed to have "twelve years of experience" in a technology that had existed for three. When pressed, he explained that he had been working with it "in spirit" before it was invented. He did not get the job. I see no reason to apply a different standard here.

[^3]: I recognize this pattern. I once did nothing for an entire weekend and then wrote forty pages of manuscript in a single Sunday evening. The results were, predictably, uneven. Several paragraphs had to be discarded. One was later used as evidence against me in a civil proceeding. The universe has a similar quality of manic, unedited output.

[^4]: At some point we must ask: if God is omniscient, why does His universe contain so many errors? If He is omnipotent, why has He not fixed them? And if He is omnipresent, why has no one ever seen Him? These are not theological questions. They are the questions you ask when someone's CV doesn't add up.
