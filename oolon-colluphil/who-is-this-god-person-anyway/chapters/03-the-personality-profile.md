# Chapter 3: The Personality Profile

*In which the author conducts a psychological assessment of the Almighty and discovers that He exhibits all the hallmarks of someone who isn't there.*

---

We have examined the qualifications. They were, to be generous, insufficient. To be accurate, they were nonexistent — which, as we shall see, is a word that comes up rather often when discussing God.

Let us now turn to personality.

Every serious institution — every corporation, every government body, every half-decent university — conducts personality assessments on candidates for important positions. These assessments are designed to identify traits that might make someone unsuitable for a role. Narcissism, for instance. Insecurity. A tendency to demand constant affirmation. A history of jealous rages. An inability to communicate clearly. A pattern of making promises and then disappearing.[^1]

I have compiled a psychological profile of God based on the available evidence — namely, every religious text ever written, every prayer ever uttered, and the general state of the universe. The results are, I'm afraid, concerning.

---

**PSYCHOLOGICAL PROFILE: Subject "God"**

**Trait 1: Neediness**

The subject requires worship. Not requests it — *requires* it. Multiple source texts describe worship as mandatory, with failure to comply resulting in punishment ranging from mild disapproval to eternal damnation, which is quite a range.

Consider what this means. The most powerful being in existence — the creator of galaxies, the architect of spacetime, the originator of every atom and every thought — needs *you*, specifically, a small mammal on a small rock in an unfashionable arm of the galaxy, to tell Him He's wonderful. Regularly. On a schedule.[^2]

This is not confidence. This is not strength. This is the behavior of someone who creates an entire universe and then stands next to it saying, "Do you like it? Do you? Do you really? You're not just saying that?"

I have known insecure people. I have been an insecure person. But I have never once created a species of sentient beings for the primary purpose of having them validate my creative choices. That is a level of neediness that transcends psychology and enters the realm of pathology.

**Trait 2: Jealousy**

The subject is, by His own admission, a "jealous God." He has stated this explicitly, in writing, in texts He reportedly authored or at least dictated. "Thou shalt have no other gods before me."

Let us pause on this.

First: the grammatical construction "no other gods" implies the existence of other gods. You do not say "no other plumbers" unless there are other plumbers. God, in the very act of demanding exclusive devotion, has acknowledged that the market has competitors. This is not monotheism. This is monopolistic practice.

Second: jealousy of whom? Of other gods who, by His own theology, do not exist. He is jealous of nothing. He is threatened by an absence. He has created an entire commandment — carved it in stone, no less — to address the danger posed by beings He claims are imaginary.

This is the behavior of someone who is not merely insecure but *delusional about the source of their insecurity*. It is the equivalent of a restaurant owner putting up a sign saying "BETTER THAN THE RESTAURANT NEXT DOOR" when there is no restaurant next door. It does not inspire confidence. It inspires the urge to eat somewhere else.[^3]

**Trait 3: Absence**

The subject has not been reliably contacted in approximately two thousand years. Possibly longer, depending on which calendar you're using and how broadly you define "reliably."

He has not answered prayers (studies confirm: prayer has no statistically measurable effect on outcomes, though it does have a measurable effect on the person praying, in the same way that talking to a photograph of someone has a measurable effect on the person talking).

He has not intervened in any crisis, catastrophe, or extinction event, of which there have been several, some of them quite severe, many involving His own allegedly chosen people.

He has not updated His terms of service, issued a correction, published an erratum, or acknowledged any of the bugs in the system, despite having — if He exists — perfect knowledge of every single one.

He has not returned calls.

This is the profile of a landlord, not a deity.

I say this with the full weight of someone who has rented property on six different planets, and I can tell you: the behavioral pattern is identical. Grand promises at the beginning. A period of apparent involvement. Then silence. Gradual decay. Ignored maintenance requests. And eventually, the dawning realization that the person who was supposed to be taking care of things has moved on, possibly to another property, possibly to another dimension, possibly to a state of nonexistence that, in retrospect, explains quite a lot about the dripping tap.

**Trait 4: Vindictiveness**

The subject has, according to His own authorized biography, drowned the entire planet (except one family and a boat full of increasingly agitated animals), destroyed two cities with fire from the sky, sent plagues of locusts, boils, and firstborn-death, turned a woman into a pillar of salt for the crime of *looking backward*, and threatened the majority of sentient life with eternal torment for the crime of not believing in Him — a condition He has made extraordinarily easy to fall into by providing no verifiable evidence of His existence.

This is not justice. This is not even punishment. This is the behavior of someone who creates a maze, doesn't put any cheese in it, and then electrocutes the mice for not finding the cheese.

**Trait 5: Inconsistency**

The subject claims to be unchanging, yet has changed His mind about: dietary laws, animal sacrifice, the acceptability of slavery, the importance of foreskin, and whether or not He likes humans. He has issued contradictory instructions to different groups and then watched them fight wars over the contradictions. He is, as far as I can determine, the only being in history to start an argument with Himself and lose.

---

I present this profile not to mock. Well — not *only* to mock. I present it because it tells us something essential.

These are not the personality traits of a supreme being. They are the personality traits of a *projection*. They are what you get when you ask a species of frightened, tribal, hierarchical primates to imagine the most powerful thing they can conceive of. They imagine themselves, but bigger. With all the same insecurities, jealousies, and tantrums, just scaled up to cosmic proportions.

God's personality is a mirror. And when you look into a mirror and see a face, you do not conclude that there is someone living inside the glass. You conclude that you are looking at yourself.

Unless you are very drunk, in which case you might have a conversation with the mirror and come away thinking you've had a religious experience. Which, I suspect, is how most religious experiences actually work.[^4]

The theologian will object: "You are describing the God of primitive texts. The true God is beyond personality, beyond human projection, beyond comprehension."

To which I reply: if God is beyond comprehension, then you cannot comprehend Him. If you cannot comprehend Him, you cannot describe Him. If you cannot describe Him, you cannot worship Him. And if you cannot worship Him — well, there goes His primary source of emotional sustenance, and we're back to the neediness problem.

You cannot have it both ways. You cannot say God is an incomprehensible mystery and also that He wants you to go to church on Sunday. You cannot say God is beyond human understanding and also that He has *opinions about shellfish*.

This is a landlord, not a deity. And like all landlords, His most defining characteristic is the one we keep coming back to, the one that explains everything else, the one that makes all the other traits moot:

He isn't here.

[^2]: On some planets, the schedule is quite specific. Twice daily, facing a particular direction, using particular words, in a particular language. This is not worship. This is a subscription service. One half expects to receive an invoice.

[^3]: I did once eat at a restaurant that had such a sign. The food was terrible. The restaurant next door, which didn't exist, would almost certainly have been better.

[^4]: I have been accused of reductionism. I accept the charge. The universe is very large, and reducing it to comprehensible size is the only way to examine it without going mad. God, if He exists, should appreciate this. He made us small. He should not be surprised that we think small thoughts.
