# Foreword

## A Note from the Publisher

*In which Conditions Press attempts to distance itself from the contents of this book while simultaneously publishing it.*

---

We at Conditions Press have published many books over the years. Books about cooking. Books about gardening. Books about the mating habits of the Arcturan Megadonkey, which we printed without hesitation despite the photographs.[^1]

We publish this book with hesitation.

Not because we doubt its quality. Mr. Colluphil is, whatever else one might say about him, a thorough thinker. His previous works — *Where God Went Wrong* and *Some More of God's Greatest Mistakes* — were, if not bestsellers, then at least best-sellers-of-the-furniture-of-bookshops-that-stocked-them, as they were frequently used to level wobbly tables and were thus indirectly responsible for a measurable increase in café stability across the western spiral arm.

No, we hesitate because of what happened after Mr. Colluphil submitted this manuscript.

Within six hours of receiving the final draft, we were contacted by the following parties, in this order:

1. Our lawyers, who advised us not to publish.
2. The Amalgamated Union of Theologians, who advised us not to publish and also not to exist.
3. A representative of the Jatravartid Church of the Great Green Arkleseizure, who sneezed at us meaningfully.
4. Seven (7) separate deities, ranging from minor household gods to one (1) major omnipotent being, all requesting — with varying degrees of politeness — that we reconsider.[^2]
5. Mr. Colluphil himself, who called to ask if we'd received the manuscript and to mention that his cat was sitting on a second copy, "just in case."

We have been advised by our legal team that publishing this book may constitute an act of theological aggression under the Conditions of Conditions Act (Sub-section 42, Paragraph: Oh Dear). We have been advised by our theological consultants that this book may cause one or more deities to experience "existential distress." We have been advised by our insurance provider that our premiums will increase by a factor of infinity.

We have also been advised — by one very worried deity who appeared in our managing editor's office as a burning filing cabinet — that "some questions are better left unasked."[^3]

To which we can only reply: we are a publishing house. Questions that are better left unasked are our entire business model.

A few notes on the text itself:

Mr. Colluphil submitted the manuscript in what he described as "final form," though it arrived covered in tea stains, marginal notes arguing with themselves, and one page that appeared to be a shopping list (we have included it as Chapter 5, as it was more coherent than the original Chapter 5). The footnotes were originally far more extensive but have, Mr. Colluphil informed us, been "gradually removed as the argument progresses, because footnotes are a kind of faith — little prayers to precision — and by the end of this book, there is nothing left to pray to."

We thought he was joking. Having read the manuscript, we are no longer sure Mr. Colluphil jokes. We think he may simply describe reality in a way that reality finds uncomfortable.

A word about the final chapter. We considered cutting it. Our editors felt it was "too quiet" and "insufficiently funny" for a work of comic philosophy. Mr. Colluphil's response, delivered by post on a card featuring a photograph of a sunset, read: "Good."

We have left it in.

To our readers: we are sorry. Not for publishing this book — we believe in the free exchange of ideas, even ideas that cause omnipotent beings to question their own existence. We are sorry because, having read it, you will not be able to unread it. And the universe, already a confusing enough place, will become slightly more so.

To any deities reading this foreword: we mean no disrespect. We are sure you exist.[^4] Please do not smite our offices. We have just had them carpeted.

To Mr. Colluphil, wherever he is: your cat is fine. He is sitting on a third copy of the manuscript. He does not seem troubled by its contents, but then, he has never seemed troubled by anything, which may make him the wisest philosopher of us all.

---

*Vroomfondel Oat III*
*Managing Director, Conditions Press*
*Maximegalon*

*P.S. — The burning filing cabinet left scorch marks on the carpet. We are sending the cleaning bill to the Almighty. We do not expect a reply.*

[^3]: The filing cabinet was later extinguished, but the question of whether a deity who communicates through office furniture can reasonably claim to be "ineffable" remains, in our view, open.

[^4]: We are not sure you exist. Please see Chapter 7.
