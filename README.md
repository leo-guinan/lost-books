# The Lost Books Collection

*A repository of literary works recovered from various corners of the galaxy, several dimensions, and at least one particularly well-organized jumble sale.*

---

## About This Collection

These texts were discovered in the ruins of the Great Library of Maximegalon, wedged between a copy of *Everything You Never Wanted to Know About Sex But Have Been Forced to Find Out* and a Conditions of Conditions leaflet from the Campaign for Real Time.

They have been digitized, translated from Conditions-of-Arrival format, and made available here for the education of beings across all known dimensions.

The publishers accept no responsibility for any existential crises, ontological collapses, or spontaneous disappearances that may result from reading these works.

---

## Currently Available Works

### The Oolon Colluphil Theological Trilogy

The famous (some would say infamous) trilogy of philosophical blockbusters that proved, via a conditions-of-conditions chain of reasoning involving a conditions-of-conditions amount of lunch, that God does not exist.

God, upon reading the proof, was so convinced that He promptly vanished in a puff of logic. He has not been seen since, though He does still owe Oolon Colluphil money for a restaurant bill on Stavromula Beta.

1. **[Where God Went Wrong](oolon-colluphil/where-god-went-wrong/README.md)** — The opening salvo. A measured, academic disappointment in the Almighty's project management.

2. **[Some More of God's Greatest Mistakes](oolon-colluphil/some-more-of-gods-greatest-mistakes/README.md)** — The emboldened sequel. Colluphil had tenure now, and he was getting reckless.

3. **[Who Is This God Person Anyway?](oolon-colluphil/who-is-this-god-person-anyway/README.md)** — The kill shot. The book that actually did the job.

## A Note on Provenance

These texts were authenticated by the Department of Conditions at the University of Maximegalon, which itself may or may not exist depending on which day of the week you ask and whether anyone has checked recently.

The authenticating professor has since retired to a small planet in the vicinity of Betelgeuse, where he breeds fish.

## Contributing

If you have recovered additional lost texts, please submit them via pull request. We accept works in all known languages, several unknown ones, and interpretive dance (video format only).

We do not accept manuscripts written in conditions-of-conditions formatting, as the last one we tried to parse caused a stack overflow in three separate dimensions.
