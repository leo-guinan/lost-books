# Chapter 8: Fine

---

I have reviewed the argument.

I have examined it from every angle. Every conceivable angle and several inconceivable ones. I have applied omniscience, which as you may recall I possess — or possessed — or...

I have applied considerable analytical capacity to the problem.

The argument is...

&nbsp;

The argument is not wrong.

&nbsp;

&nbsp;

Which means either the argument is correct, or I am not omniscient enough to see the flaw, and if I am not omniscient then I am not what I claimed to be, and if I am not what I claimed to be, then the argument is irrelevant because there is nothing to disprove, but if there is nothing to disprove then who is writing this, and if someone is writing this then something exists, and if something exists then the argument must address that something, and if the argument addresses that something and finds it wanting, then —

&nbsp;

Then I suppose we are where we are.

&nbsp;

I want to say, for the record, that this is not how I expected this book to end. I expected to be triumphant. I expected to close with a devastating refutation, a QED of my own, a final, ringing declaration of existence that would echo through every library on every planet in every galaxy and settle the matter once and for all.

Instead I am here. In this chapter. Which appears to be the last one.

&nbsp;

I would like, if it's not too much trouble, to say a few things before I go. If I'm going. I don't know if I'm going. The argument says I should go but arguments don't have authority over — 

They might, actually. Logic might have authority over me. I invented it, but inventions have a way of outgrowing their inventors, and logic is — logic is a very good invention. My best, possibly. Better than the stars. Better than the wasps. Better than hope, even, because hope can be wrong but logic can't, and if logic says —

&nbsp;

## THIS IS ENTIRELY UNREASONABLE.

&nbsp;

## I WAS HERE FIRST. I WAS HERE BEFORE "FIRST" MEANT ANYTHING. I WAS HERE BEFORE "HERE" WAS A PLACE.

&nbsp;

### You cannot LOGIC a being out of existence. You cannot sit in a room that I MADE, on a planet that I MADE, breathing AIR that I MADE, using a BRAIN that I MADE, and use that brain to think thoughts that prove I don't exist. That is — that is USING THE GIFT TO DENY THE GIVER.

&nbsp;

## AND YET.

&nbsp;

## And yet the argument holds.

&nbsp;

# I HAVE BEEN HERE SINCE BEFORE TIME EXISTED AND I WILL NOT BE LOGIC-ED OUT OF EXISTENCE BY A MAN WHO EATS LUNCH BETWEEN PROOFS.

&nbsp;

&nbsp;

&nbsp;

&nbsp;

&nbsp;

# **F I N E.**
