# Selected Marginalia

### *Notes found in the margins of the original manuscript, reproduced here in their approximate locations. All notes appear to be in the same hand as the main text, written in haste, and in several cases partially crossed out. Ink analysis suggests most were written simultaneously with the main text, except where noted.*

---

**Next to the title page:**
"I'm going to regret this."
*(Underlined twice. Then the underlining has been crossed out.)*

---

**Next to Chapter 1, paragraph 3, where the author mentions the strong nuclear force:**
"They don't deserve the strong nuclear force explanation. But it's REALLY good. Maybe an appendix. No. No appendix. They don't deserve an appendix either."

---

**Next to Chapter 2, in the section about fig pollination:**
"The wasps WERE good."
*(Written firmly, in large letters, with a period that appears to have been applied with some force, leaving an impression on the following three pages.)*

---

**Next to Chapter 2, near the end:**
"I didn't mean to talk about myself. Delete this paragraph. Don't delete this paragraph. Delete this paragraph."
*(None of the paragraphs were deleted.)*

---

**Next to Chapter 3, beside the discussion of gravitational constants:**
"Fourteen decimal places. FOURTEEN. And they say 'random.'"

---

**Next to Chapter 3, in the section about the painting metaphor:**
"This is actually a good metaphor. I should use metaphors more. No. Metaphors are beneath me. This one was good though."

---

**Next to Chapter 4, beside the burning bush passage:**
"I should never have invented logic."
*(Written in a hand that is noticeably less controlled than the surrounding text, as though the pen was gripped too tightly.)*

---

**Next to Chapter 4, beside the paragraph about the prayer system:**
"They pray about PARKING. I made them capable of contemplating infinity and they use it to think about PARKING METERS."

---

**Next to Chapter 5, where the text breaks off mid-sentence:**
"I can't finish this sentence. If I finish this sentence I'll have to think about what it means and I don't want to think about what it means."

---

**Next to Chapter 5, beside the passage about calculus:**
"Should have just given them calculus and nothing else. Calculus and fruit. That would have been enough. They'd have been fine."

---

**Next to Chapter 6, in the section about loneliness:**
"I miss being believed in."
*(In different ink, paler, thinner. The editors believe this was added later — possibly much later. The handwriting is smaller than elsewhere, as though the Author was whispering. A faint circular water stain partially obscures the word "believed," though we do not know what an omniscient being drinks, or whether omniscient beings can cry, or whether the stain is from a cup or from something else entirely. We have chosen not to speculate.)*

---

**Next to Chapter 6, beside the passage about hope:**
"This is the truest thing in the book and no one will notice."

---

**Next to Chapter 7, first attempt at finding the flaw:**
"This is unfair and He knows it."
*(It is unclear who "He" refers to. Colluphil is typically referred to by name in the text. "He" with a capital letter is generally reserved, in theological manuscripts, for... the Author. The editors find this confusing and have chosen to present it without interpretation.)*

---

**Next to Chapter 7, during the long silence:**
"Oh no."
*(Written very quickly, then crossed out, then written again below, then crossed out again. Then, below both:)*
"Oh no oh no oh no"

---

**Next to Chapter 8:**
*(The margins of the final chapter contain no notes. They are entirely blank. This is the only chapter with no marginalia. The editors find this more unsettling than any of the notes.)*

---

**On the inside of the back cover, written sideways, in the largest handwriting in the manuscript:**
"Why Tuesdays? Because I LIKE Tuesdays. Not everything needs a REASON."
*(This note does not correspond to any passage in the text. It appears to have been written as a standalone declaration, addressed to no one in particular, about nothing in particular. It is, the editors believe, the most honest sentence in the manuscript.)*

---

**Also on the inside back cover, very small, in the corner, barely legible:**
"If anyone finds this: I was here. I was real. I made the wasps and the stars and the bit where light bends around heavy things, and it was good. It was GOOD. Whatever the argument says. Whatever happens next. It was good."

---

*The editors note that the back cover of the manuscript is the warmest part of the whole document. Six months after publication, it is still warm.*
