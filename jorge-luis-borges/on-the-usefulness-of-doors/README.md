# On the Usefulness of Doors

### Jorge Luis Borges

#### (*De la utilidad de las puertas*, ca. 1957)

---

*Recovered from a second-hand bookshop in Buenos Aires, wedged inside a copy of the Encyclopaedia Britannica, Volume 4 (Cassius to Dodo). The pages were unnumbered. The text was handwritten in violet ink. The bookshop owner claimed he had never seen it before, though it appeared to have been there for some time, as the pages had acquired the particular amber tone of paper that has been patient.*

*The manuscript was discovered on the 14th of March, 2024, by a graduate student searching for an entry on Coleridge. She found Borges instead, which is a thing that happens more often than one might suppose.*

*Each chapter is shorter than the one before it. This is not an accident. The book is a series of rooms, each smaller than the last, and you are walking through them.
