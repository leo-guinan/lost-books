# VII. The Last Door

There is a door at the end of every corridor, and a corridor beyond every door, and this is what we call living — the passage from room to room, each one smaller or larger than the last, none of them final, until the last one, which is not a room at all but the absence of rooms, which is another way of saying: the door that opens onto no threshold, no room, no self to walk through it. We call this death. But perhaps it is only the first door we walk through without looking back.
